Creating Volunteer personas?

Volunteer archetypes

***Archetypes differ from personas in important ways. These are common patterns in stories and mythologies (like the hero, rebel, or everyman) that are easy to identify and relate to. However, they are not prescriptive and they do not offer specific customer details.

The value of archetypes is that they help drive aspirational models by evoking creativity and imagination.

How to Use Personas and Archetypes to Drive Shared Understanding and Digital Strategy (Method.com)

Why

  • Forward-thinking and aspirational ‚Äì who do we want to cater to, including and especially those who aren‚Äôt on wiki yet?
  • Higher-level focus on the why and future state (over what they do now).
  • More inclusive & empathetic
  • Flexible and adaptable to be shared across teams and across different user journeys
  • Storytelling device that connects with and moves people

Which volunteers do we mean?

Volunteers on Wikimedia can mean content editors, admins, stewards, bot writers, developers, event organisers, etc ‚Äì anyone who spends their free time participating in growing or shaping content on our projects.

How might we focus and prioritise the types of volunteers that we want retain and those we want more of - esp. those who don‚Äôt see the value prop or know that volunteering on wiki is even a thing?

Archetype example: The Passionate Advocate

If we think this persona is vital for generations to come, how might we better cater to them across different parts of their wiki journey?

  • What gets more Passionate Advocates to edit?
  • How can code-savvy Passionate Advocates create tools to surface knowledge gaps?
  • How might Passionate Advocates drive donations?
